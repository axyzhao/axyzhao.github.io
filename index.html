Do Offline Metrics Predict Online Performance in Recommender Systems?
Karl Krauth, Sarah Dean*, Alex Zhao*, Wenshuo Guo*, Mihaela Curmei, Benjamin Recht, Michael I. Jordan
Under review, 2020
arXiv

Recommender systems operate in an inherently dynamical setting. Past recommendations influence future behavior, including which data points are observed and how user preferences change. However, experimenting in production systems with real user dynamics is often infeasible, and existing simulation-based approaches have limited scale. As a result, many state-of-the-art algorithms are designed to solve supervised learning problems, and progress is judged only by offline metrics. In this work we investigate the extent to which offline metrics predict online performance by evaluating eleven recommenders across six controlled simulated environments. We provide the environments and recommenders described in this paper as Reclab: an extensible ready-to-use simulation framework at RecLab.

AugPrune: Robust Network Pruning via Augmented Data
Alex Zhao, Yaoqing Yang, Xiangyu Yue, Zhuang Liu, Elicia Ye, Kurt Keutzer, Kannan Ramchandran
Pending submission, 2020

Deep convolutional networks are vulnerable to corruptions that frequently occur in natural images, often called out-of-distribution (OOD) transforms. Deep convolutional networks also require a large number of parameters, which makes them difficult to deploy when computing resources are scarce. To mitigate these problems, we propose AugPrune, a new technique for removing channels that correspond to non-robust and sensitive features in a convolutional network. AugPrune uses a novel pruning algorithm on neural networks trained with existing data augmentation techniques to learn smaller, more robust models.

Attention Gets You the Right Size and Fit in Fashion
Karl Hajjar, Julia Lasserre, Alex Zhao, Reza Shirvany,
Workshop on Recommender Systems in Fashion, Recsys, 2020
Workshop page

Size recommendation systems capable of estimating a customer’s size in thousands of available brands and categories ahead of purchase time are deemed invaluable in dramatically reducing the number of returns related to size and fit. We present a flexible and at scale size recommendation approach that overcomes some limitations of current state-of-the-art work by building upon the recent advances in natural language processing and casting the size recommendation problem as a kind of “translation” problem (from articles to sizes) using attention-based deep learning models for size and fit prediction.

Integrative Complexity in Election Cycles: Lessons from American Presidential Candidates
Alex Zhao, Jennifer Jones, Kristen Monroe
Oral paper at Annual Conference of the International Society on Political Psychology, 2017
Medium post

Using the concept of integrative complexity, a psychometric developed to measure the degree of information processing capacity, this paper analyzes differences between Republican and Democratic presidential candidates on domestic and foreign policy by coding a set of interview transcripts (N = 77). Results showed that, in terms of foreign policy, Democratic candidates are significantly more complex than Republicans; in domestic policy, Democrats are only marginally more complex. These results may be due to differences in party platform, the challenger/incumbent distinction, or the characteristics of the particular candidates in the recent election cycle. In the last section of the paper, some limitations to the application of integrative complexity as a conceptual tool for the analysis of interview transcripts and potential for future research are discussed.
